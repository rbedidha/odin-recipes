Grilled Cheese

Description

A grilled cheese sandwich is a simple, comforting dish made by placing cheese between two slices of bread and grilling or toasting it until the bread is golden brown and crispy, and the cheese is melted. It's often cooked with butter on the outside of the bread to enhance flavor and crispiness.

Ingredients

  • Bread
  • Cheese
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Spread butter on one side of each slice of bread.
  2. Place cheese between the unbuttered sides of the bread, so the buttered sides face out.
  3. Heat a skillet over medium heat. Place the sandwich in the skillet and cook until golden brown, about 2â€“4 minutes per side, pressing slightly with a spatula.
  4. Once both sides are crisp and the cheese is melted, remove from heat, slice, and serve warm.
